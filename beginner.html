129 Essential Films for Beginners in a life

Pulp Fiction (1994)

Genre:Drama, dark comedy

Tags:American Classic, gangster moovie, multiple stories

The Quentin Tarratino's film the best know. An American classic which featuring Sameul L Jackson and John Travolta as well as Uma Thurman into a dark comedy with crazy characters in the The world of American gangsters . Probably one of my favourite film made by perhaps one of the greatest directors of all time.

Interstellar (2014)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Adventure, Drama

Tags: Space Travel, Time Dilation, Emotional Core

A modern sci-fi epic that combines scientific ambition with human emotion. Great for beginners to explore storytelling beyond Earth. A film that shows the importance of ecology

Schindler List (1993)

Genre: Drama, Historic, emotion, War

Tags: WW2, deportation, cruelty, sadness, violence, kindness of one man

My favourite Steven Spielberg's film. The horror of the war is brought to life incredibly well on screen. However the human sanctity also exists by one man Oscar Schindler.

Parasite (2019)

Genre: Drama, Thriller, dark comedy

Tags: class warfare , suspense, emotion, violence

One of the best film of the South Korean cinema made by Bong Joon-ho. This flim explores class divide through the gripping tale of two family, one wealthy and the other struggling to survive. The tension of the film will give you some emotion.

Goodfellas (1990)

Genre: Drama, gangster movie

Tags: suspense, emotion, gagnsters, betrayal, violence

My favourite Scorsese's film. This film is for me the ultimate gangster film. Led by an incredible Joe Pesci this film mixes violence dark humor and an incredible scenario based on a true gangster story. Absolute cinema.

Shining (1980)

Genre: horror, thriller, emotion

Tags: Stephen King, psychologic, paranormal, madness

The Shining is a haunting psychological horror that follows a family's descent into madness at an isolated, snowbound hotel. As supernatural forces stir, the father unravels, turning on his wife and son in chilling, unforgettable ways.

Spirited away (2001)

Genre: Drama, fantastic, animation

Tags: loneliness, work slavery, surnatural, emotion

Spirited Away is a breathtaking, magical adventure that you’ll fall in love with, just like I did. It follows a young girl, Chihiro, who must navigate a mysterious spirit world to save her parents and find her courage. Every frame is pure wonder, blending heart, fantasy, and unforgettable characters in a way that feels like a dream you never want to leave.

Million dollar baby (2005)

Genre: Drama

Tags: guilt, handicap, the meaning of death, euthanasia, emotion

Million Dollar Baby is a deeply moving film that I truly love,it’s powerful, raw, and stays with you. It tells the story of a determined woman chasing her boxing dream and the bond she forms with a hardened trainer. What starts as inspiration turns heartbreakingly emotional, delivering a story of love, sacrifice, and strength.

No Country for old men (2007)

Genre: Thriller, Drama, Western

Tags: hitman, psychopath, money, death, epicness

My favourite Cohen brothers's films which tells us a story of grit with modern western storytelling. Set in the desolate Texas desert, it follows a hunter who stumbles upon a drug deal gone wrong and a ruthless killer who comes after him. With its epic cat-and-mouse chase and chilling villain, it feels like a brutal, poetic western for the ages.

The Truman Show (1998)

Genre: Drama, Sci-Fi

Tags: identity, surveillance, media, self-discovery

An imaginative and emotional story that invites you to question reality, identity, and freedom. Jim Carrey shows his dramatic depth in a film that's both accessible and profound.

La La Land (2016)

Genre: Musical, Romance, Drama

Tags: dreams, love, ambition, music, Los Angeles

A dazzling modern musical that balances joy with melancholy. Its visual style and unforgettable soundtrack make it a perfect introduction to romantic, emotionally resonant cinema.

The Big Lebowski (1998)

Genre: Comedy, Crime

Tags: bowling, absurdity, mistaken identity, cult classic

The Dude abides. A hilariously weird trip through Los Angeles that introduces the Coen brothers' unique humor and storytelling style.

Ratatouille (2007)

Genre: Animation, Comedy, Drama

Tags: cooking, identity, passion, underdog, France

A rat who wants to be a chef in Paris? Surprisingly inspiring. A heartwarming celebration of passion, art, and creativity, no matter who you are.

Gran Torino (2008)

Genre: Drama

Tags: racism, redemption, community, masculinity

A deeply human story of growth and unexpected connection. Clint Eastwood directs and stars in one of his most emotionally resonant roles.

Titanic (1997)

Genre: Romance, Disaster, Drama

Tags: love, class, tragedy, historical, romance, box office

An iconic love story set against one of the most famous tragedies in history. A visually sweeping, emotionally gripping film that’s unforgettable.

Forrest Gump (1994)

Genre: Drama, Romance

Tags: American history, destiny, love, innocence

A touching and whimsical journey through decades of American life told through the eyes of an unlikely hero. Heartfelt and endlessly quotable, a perfect entry point to classic Hollywood storytelling.

Mulholland Drive (2001)

Genre: Mystery, Drama, Thriller

Tags: identity, Hollywood, dreams, confusion, illusion, David Lynch

A haunting puzzle of love, memory, and illusion. Not an easy watch, but unforgettable once it locks into your imagination.

Gladiator (2000)

Genre: Action, Drama, History

Tags: revenge, ancient Rome, heroism, spectacle

A grand and gritty epic of justice and vengeance. Russell Crowe brings fire and soul to a warrior's journey for honor.

Paths of Glory (1957)

Genre: War, Drama

Tags: injustice, war, sacrifice, leadership

Kirk Douglas gives a powerful performance in this anti-war masterpiece. Intimate and scathing. A timeless film.

Star Wars: A New Hope (1977)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Adventure, Fantasy

Tags: space opera, rebellion, heroism, legacy

A timeless adventure that introduced the world to a galaxy far, far away. An ideal first step into iconic pop culture cinema.

Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back (1980)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Action

Tags: destiny, family, darkness, redemption

Darker, deeper, and even more iconic than the first. A sequel that elevated Star Wars to a true epic saga.

Star Wars: Return of the Jedi (1983)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Action

Tags: redemption, legacy, conclusion, hope

An emotionally satisfying conclusion to one of the most beloved trilogies ever. Good vs evil at its grandest.

A Clockwork Orange (1971)

Genre: Crime, Sci-Fi, Drama

Tags: violence, control, dystopia, ethics

Provocative, disturbing, and thought-provoking. A masterclass in tone and control. Not for the faint of heart.

Alien (1979)

Genre: Horror, Sci-Fi

Tags: space, survival, fear, isolation

Claustrophobic and terrifying, Alien is sci-fi horror at its absolute best. Ripley is a timeless hero of strength and wit.

Invictus (2009)

Genre: Biography, Drama, Sport

Eyes Wide Shut (1999)

Genre: Drama, Mystery

Tags: marriage, identity, ritual, secrecy

A hypnotic and unsettling journey through sexual obsession and disconnection. One of Kubrick’s most mysterious and debated films.

Thelma & Louise (1991)

Genre: Adventure, Drama, Crime

Tags: freedom, feminism, friendship, rebellion

An iconic road movie that’s fierce, funny, and fearless. A must-see for its characters and its place in cinema history.

The Prestige (2006)

Genre: Mystery, Drama, Sci-Fi

Tags: obsession, magic, rivalry, sacrifice

A brilliant narrative puzzle of ambition and deceit. Nolan takes you deep into illusion and truth, with a killer ending.

The Bridges of Madison County (1995)

Genre: Romance, Drama

Tags: longing, missed chances, mature love, sacrifice

Jurassic Park (1993)

Genre: Adventure, Sci-Fi, Thriller

Tags: dinosaurs, science, chaos, spectacle

A breathtaking blend of awe and terror. Spielberg's mastery of suspense and wonder makes this a must-see cinematic thrill ride.

Barton Fink (1991)

Blade Runner (1982)
